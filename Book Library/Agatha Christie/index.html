Agatha Christie - Biography and Works

Introduction:

    Basic Information:
  • Full Name: Agatha Mary Clarissa Miller
  • Birth: September 15, 1890, Torquay, Devon, England
  • Death: January 12, 1976, Wallingford, Oxfordshire, England
  • Occupation: Writer
  • Genres: Mystery, Detective Fiction, Thriller

Early Life:

Agatha Christie was born in Torquay, Devon, England, in 1890. She was the youngest of three children in the Miller family. Her father, Frederick Miller, was an American stockbroker, and her mother, Clara Miller, was a British socialite. Christie was educated at home and developed a love for reading and writing from a young age. She began writing poems and short stories as a child and continued to do so throughout her life.

Major Works:

Agatha Christie is best known for her detective novels and short stories, which feature iconic characters such as Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple. Some of her most famous works include:

  • der of Roger Ackroyd (1926)
  • And Then There Were None (1939)
  • Death on the Nile (1937)
  • The A.B.C. Murders (1936)
  • Five Little Pigs (1942)
  • Crooked House (1949)

Legacy:

Agatha Christie’s works have had a lasting impact on the mystery and detective fiction genres. Her intricate plots, clever twists, and memorable characters have made her one of the most popular and widely read authors in the world. Christie’s works continue to be adapted into films, television series, and stage productions, and her influence can be seen in the works of many contemporary mystery writers.
